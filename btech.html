What is unique about our BTech Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) Admission Process?

At NU, it is important for us to find the right students and we also believe that it is important for the students to find the right institution. We believe that the parent/guardian forms the crucial third pillar without whom the process of selection would remain incomplete. Hence we have devised, what we believe is, an apt admission process that gives an opportunity for the students as well as their parents/guardians to engage with us.

The process of BTech Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) admission is called Admission Interaction Process or AIP for short. The AIP consists of three parts.

Admission Interaction Process (AIP) (show in a flowchart)

1.Questionnaire stating student’s aspirations and reasons for choosing Computer Science & Engineering as a career option.

2. The applicant will appear for NUET which is conducted by NU for admission to its Technology Programmes. It consists of questions drawn from Physics/ Chemistry/ Mathematics and/or Biology.

The applicants will undergo the Personal Counselling Interaction (PCI) either face-to-face or through video conferencing.

The following will be assessed during the PCI:

1.English communication skills – written & oral

2. Interest & hobbies

3. Degree of self-awareness

4. Out-of-the-box thinking

5. Reason for the choice of stream

6. Behavior and attitude

7. Overall personality

NU also believes that due weightage and recognition should be given to the academic learning the student has undergone even before taking up B Tech admission. Hence multiple components of academics including class X performance, class XII performance and performance in a recognized engineering entrance tests are considered.

Note: In case the student has decided not to appear in an engineering entrance test, s/he has to undertake the NIIT UNIVERSITY ENGINEERING TEST (NUET).It is a mandatory test for every candidate who has not appeared in JEE-Main/ BITSAT/SAT/NEET/ Any State Engineering Entrance Examination

This is an objective type online computer based test.

The test duration is 60 minutes.

The candidate has to choose Physics, Chemistry & Mathematics for CSE

Applying for NIIT University’s BTech Computer Science & Engineering Admissions

1. ADMISSION MODES

NU offers admission for the Bachelors and Integrated Masters Programmes via two modes:

1. Early Admission Mode

2. Regular Admission Mode

EARLY ADMISSION MODE

NU offers Early Admissions to students appearing for their Class XII Board Examinations in March-April 2019 as also to those who have taken their Class XII Board examination not earlier than March 2017. The EAD process is particularly suitable for those who are clear about pursuing engineering or management studies or/and multidisciplinary curriculum leading to post-graduate career or those willing to set up their own start-up ventures after graduation. This is also beneficial to NU to spread out its admission process since it gives more time for in-depth interaction with the applicants which is a vital part of the NU selection process.

The applicants seeking admission in this mode can also apply for scholarship under the NU Scholar Search Programme. There are limited seats for which B Tech admission is being granted under the Early Admission Mode. The admission will be granted on a first come first served basis to only those applicants who meet all the admission criteria as defined for B Tech admission.

BENEFITS OF EARLY ADMISSION

Early Admissions ensure that the student can be stress free and can focus on exceling in their Class XII Board examinations.

NU supports the selected Early Admissions students for their Class XII exams through the ‘Study Smart Initiative’.

Opportunity to attend Camp Future, a two-day getaway at NU campus, designed to give students and their parents, a glimpse of the NU life, as guests of NIIT University.

EAD students get financial benefit in terms of their acceptance fee paid by EAD students gets adjusted towards security fee deposit payable the time of enrolment.

Early Admission students benefits from Early Registration on the campus – 7 days earlier and attend Head start Boot Camp. This Boot Camp is before the formal registration date, which commences with NU Resonance – a unique NU Induction program.

NU Scholar search programme offers limited number of meritorious scholarships on a first-come-first-served basis. EAD students have additional advantage of securing these scholarships by early admission offer acceptance.

TIMELINES

The application forms for the EAD mode will be available from Sept 25, 2018 onwards.

The last date to submit applications is –

1. Regular Admission - Round 1 - May 15, 2019

Students are strongly advised to apply and undergo the admission process at the earliest.

REGULAR ADMISSION MODE (RAD)

NU offers admission into its prestigious Bachelor’s and Integrated Master Programmes under Regular Admission Mode (RAD) for eligible applicants who have appeared in Class XII Board Examinations in March/April 2019 or have successfully completed Class XII in March 2018 or 2017.

There are limited seats for which admission is being granted under the Regular Application Mode. The admission will be granted on a first come first served basis to only those applicants who meet the admission criteria.

TIMELINES

The application form for the RAD MODE will be available from April 2019.

B TECH LATERAL ENTRY

The following are the guidelines for lateral admission to the 2nd year for those who have completed B Tech 1st Year from another institution.

NU will accept lateral entry candidates to the B Tech Programme at the end of first semester of first year or at the end of first year and not later

Applicants are required to submit:

Mark sheets and/or Grade sheets of the first semester or the first two semesters as applicable Copy of syllabus certified by college/university

Reasons for seeking lateral entry

B Tech Application Form via online or offline mode

The above information will be examined by a competent body constituted for the purpose. If the applicant if found to be eligible for lateral admission, s/he will need to appear for AIP.

There is no automatic waiver of credits accumulated at the candidate’s institution. The final credit waiver will be arrived at after examining the commonality in course content of NU and the applicant’s institution.
